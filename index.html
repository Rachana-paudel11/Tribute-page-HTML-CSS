Pasang Lhamu Sherpa

1961 - 1993

First Nepalese Woman to Climb the Summit of Mount Everest

Pasang Lhamu Sherpa was thefirst Nepali woman to summit Mount Everest. Born on December 10, 1961, in Solukhumbu, Nepal, she was a courageous mountaineer who broke gender barriers in a male-dominated field. After several attempts, she successfully reached the summit of Everest on April 22, 1993, but tragically lost her life during the descent due to bad weather. Pasang Lhamu is remembered as a national heroine of Nepal, and her legacy continues to inspire women across the country to pursue their dreams. In her honor, a statue stands in Kathmandu, and various roads, schools, and institutions bear her name.
